publications

Systems composed of many interacting parts often exhibit dramatically different properties from their components:

“There’s no love in a carbon atom, no hurricane in a water molecule, [and] no financial collapse in a dollar bill.”

I study how complex, emergent phenomena arise from simple microscopic rules within the framework of equilibrium statistical mechanics. Specifically, I use probabilistic and combinatorial methods to rigorously characterize the Gibbs measures of various idealized physical systems across different temperatures, densities, and other parameters.

2026

  1. Unified criteria for crystallization in hard-core lattice systems with applications to polyomino fluids and chiral mixtures
    Qidong He
    2026
    Abs arXiv

    I generalize the previous criteria for high-density crystallization in hard-core lattice systems to allow for (a) a general measure of local order and (b) multiple translationally inequivalent particle shapes. As a direct consequence, every polyomino admitting a finite number of tilings (with rotations and possibly mirror images) crystallizes at high densities.

2025

  1. Extended regime of nematic order in an interacting monomer-dimer model of Heilmann and Lieb
    Qidong He
    2025
    Abs arXiv

    I prove that a two-dimensional model for liquid crystals introduced by Heilmann–Lieb (1979) exhibits nematic order over a much larger parameter regime than previously known (Jauslin–Lieb, 2018) by adapting the proof strategy developed by Hadas–Peled (2025) for the 2×2 hard square model.

  2. Liquid-vapor transition in a model of a continuum particle system with finite-range modified Kac pair potential
    Qidong He, Ian Jauslin, Joel Lebowitz, and Ron Peled
    2025
    Abs arXiv

    We prove that a class of continuous particle systems with mesoscopically tailored, finite-range pair potentials exhibits liquid-vapor transitions by verifying a criterion of Dobrushin–Shlosman (1981).

  3. Upper bounds for the connective constant of weighted self-avoiding walks
    Qidong He
    Journal of Physics A: Mathematical and Theoretical, 2025
    Abs DOI arXiv Code

    Extending an earlier work by Alm (1993), I develop a method for deriving upper bounds on the connective constant of weighted self-avoiding walks, with implications for the convergence of the corresponding anisotropic generating functions. A Python implementation of the method is publicly available.

  4. Analyticity for locally stable hard-core gases via recursion
    Qidong He
    Journal of Statistical Physics, 2025
    Abs DOI arXiv

    Extending the recursion method developed by Michelen–Perkins (2025), I expand the known range of activities for which a continuum system of hard spheres with an additional attractive pair interaction is in the gas phase. In the high-temperature regime, the improvement surpasses the classical Penrose–Ruelle bound by at least a factor of e^2.

    Qidong He and Ian Jauslin
    Journal of Statistical Physics, 2024
    Abs DOI arXiv

    We prove that a large class of hard-core lattice particle models crystallize at high densities, using a novel characterization of local order based on discrete Voronoi diagrams.
